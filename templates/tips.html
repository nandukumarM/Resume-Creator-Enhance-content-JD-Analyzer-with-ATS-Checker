Resume Tips & Best Practices

Expert advice to make your resume ATS-friendly and stand out

DO's - Best Practices

  • Use Standard Fonts: Stick to Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman (10-12pt)
  • Include Keywords: Match job description keywords naturally in your resume
  • Use Action Verbs: Start bullet points with strong verbs (Led, Developed, Achieved)
  • Quantify Achievements: Use numbers and percentages (Increased sales by 35%)
  • Keep it Concise: 1-2 pages maximum, focus on relevant experience
  • Use Clear Headings: Professional Summary, Experience, Education, Skills
  • Include Contact Info: Phone, email, LinkedIn, location (city/state)
  • Save as PDF: PDFs preserve formatting across all systems

DON'Ts - Common Mistakes

  • Don't Use Tables/Graphics: ATS systems can't read complex formatting
  • Don't Use Headers/Footers: ATS may skip this content entirely
  • Don't Keyword Stuff: Keep it natural, don't repeat words excessively
  • Don't Use Fancy Fonts: Avoid script, decorative, or unusual fonts
  • Don't Include Photos: Unless required, skip profile pictures
  • Don't Use Abbreviations: Spell out acronyms first, then use abbreviation
  • Don't List References: "Available upon request" is unnecessary
  • Don't Use Passive Language: Replace "Responsible for" with action verbs

ATS Optimization Tips

  • Mirror Job Description: Use exact phrases from the job posting
  • Use Standard Section Titles: Avoid creative headings like "Where I've Been"
  • Include Both Acronyms & Full Forms: "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)"
  • List Skills in Context: Show how you used skills in experience section
  • Use Industry Keywords: Research common terms in your field
